Interpretation

HexHawk is differentiated, not dominant.Its best current story is a local-first evidence/reporting workbench with explicit authority boundaries. It should not claim it beats IDA/Ghidra/Binary Ninja on mature decompiler ecosystems.
Release trust is the main commercial blocker.The June 20 MSI extraction and NSIS install smokes passed, including WebView2Loader.dll in the NSIS payload, but the artifacts are still NotSigned. Public-trusted signing, hosted updater validation, and full exact-artifact export parity remain gating items.
AI must stay bounded.AETHERFRAME/Forge and NEXUS are positioning strengths only because they are policy-gated and non-authoritative. GYRE remains the sole verdict authority; NEST remains evidence orchestration.

Next Moves To Improve The Competitive Story

1. Release trust

Code-sign exe/MSI/NSIS, publish matching metadata, verify hosted hashes/signatures, and rerun native GUI proof on exact signed artifacts.

2. Analysis depth

Continue TALON decompiler maturity, STRIKE live-debugger proof, ECHO correlation, and exploitability-mode advisory slices without turning them into GYRE verdict truth.

3. Buyer proof

Produce narrow, reproducible case studies showing time saved, evidence quality, report repeatability, and export-authority parity against real analyst workflows.
