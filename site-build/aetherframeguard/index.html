AetherFrameGuard

Measure first. Change only what was described. Test again.

AetherFrameGuard is separate from HexHawk. It helps a player test CS2 and PC performance changes without guessing by showing the measurement source, the setting scope, the backup/restart notes, and what still was not proven.

1. Measure

Input: a short FPS and PC capture. Output: what was detected, what was measured, source/timestamp, and what failed to measure.

2. Apply described settings

Use it when: you accept the shown scope and backup notes. Output: the exact CS2 and Windows gaming settings attempted plus warnings. Limit: settings require re-test evidence.

3. Re-test

Input: a new gameplay-like benchmark. Output: comparison against Baseline, Latest, and Best observed. Limit: one run is not enough to prove a lasting improvement.

Current caveat

Live FPS proof depends on CS2, PresentMon, and the PC state at that moment. Logs should say exactly what was captured, what changed, whether CS2 must be relaunched, and what remains unknown.
